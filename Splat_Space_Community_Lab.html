Splat Space Community Lab

Inside the Splat Space Community Lab

Splat Space is a 501c3 non-profit offering members access to tools, machines and space to build hobby projects or start freelance businesses. The diverse member group includes professionals, hackers, hobbyists, engineers, artists, retirees and students. The space embodies the DIY ethos in all operations, being maintained and organized with the sole support of the membership.

Splat Space offers a few types of memberships which all offer access to the space and tools.

You can see more of what is available on the Space page.

Facilities and Equipment

  • Biology Community Lab: Shared space for biotechnology and synthetic biology projects
  • 3D Printing: FDM and resin printers for rapid prototyping
  • Electronics: Stations for circuit design, breadboarding, and soldering
  • Metal Working: Tools for machining, cutting, and fabrication
  • Wood Working: Full shop with table saws, band saws, and hand tools
  • Textiles: Sewing machines and embroidery equipment
  • Leather Work: Specialized tools for leather crafting
  • Laser Cutting: CO2 laser cutters for engraving and cutting various materials
  • Vinyl Cutting: Precision cutting for stickers and signage
  • Large Format Printing: Professional-grade printing capabilities

The foundational work that enabled Valonia biotechnology was performed by the SPLAT Space Community Lab team, who completed the initial genomic sequencing (May 2026) and proof-of-concept studies (November 2026) that demonstrated the feasibility of this platform.

The initial research that established Valonia ventricosa as a biotechnology platform was conducted by the SPLAT Space Community Lab, a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization dedicated to advancing biotechnology and synthetic biology research. The team brought together expertise in marine biology, genetic engineering, bioprocess engineering, and biotechnology applications.

In November 2025, the team applied for grant funding with the goal of demonstrating the transformative potential of Valonia ventricosa as a biotechnology platform. Their work successfully achieved the following milestones:

  • May 2026: Completion of genomic sequencing and initial genetic characterization, establishing the foundation for genetic engineering applications
  • November 2026: Demonstration of proof-of-concept for enzyme production and validation of Valonia as a viable biotechnology platform

Through this foundational work, the team established Valonia ventricosa as a novel, sustainable platform for biomanufacturing that leverages the organism's unique properties‚Äîits unprecedented size, accessible cytoplasm, and photosynthetic metabolism‚Äîto enable new approaches in pharmaceutical production, environmental monitoring, and biotechnology education.

The SPLAT Space Community Lab's commitment to open science, community engagement, and developing biotechnology solutions that are both scientifically innovative and socially responsible laid the groundwork for the global deployment of Valonia technology. Their research on Valonia ventricosa represented a convergence of fundamental biology, applied engineering, and practical biotechnology applications that has had lasting impact on the field.
